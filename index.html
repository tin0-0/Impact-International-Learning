Our Story

About Impact International Learning

Founded from a simple yet profound observation made during early teaching years in Zimbabwe, Impact International Learning was born from a desire to create meaningful change for learners who went unnoticed.

In 2012, many learners faced academic, social, and behavioural challenges while awareness of learning differences and developmental disabilities was limited. Today, Impact International Learning serves as a platform for education, training, supervision, consultation, and professional development.

We believe every individual deserves the opportunity to learn, grow, and thrive — regardless of their circumstances or challenges.

Our Mission

To positively impact humanity by empowering individuals, families, educators, and professionals through evidence-based learning, inclusive practices, and ethical behaviour support.

Our Vision

To be a globally trusted provider of education, training, consultation, supervision, and behaviour support services that empower individuals, families, and professionals to achieve meaningful and sustainable outcomes.

Meet the Founder

Regai Peter Zata

IBA QASP-S SEN Teacher Behaviour Analyst UAE MOE Licensed

Regai Peter Zata is a UAE Ministry of Education licensed Special Educational Needs (SEN) Teacher, Behaviour Analyst, and Professional Trainer dedicated to improving the lives of individuals with diverse learning and developmental needs.

With experience across mainstream schools, autism centres, and rehabilitation settings, he combines evidence-based educational practices with behavioural science to create meaningful and lasting outcomes for learners, families, and professionals.

Currently serving as a Special Education Needs Teacher at a mainstream IB school in the UAE, Regai specializes in designing individualized learning pathways, developing effective support plans, and helping learners access education in ways that build confidence, independence, and long-term success.

Regai holds a Bachelor of Science in Psychology and is currently pursuing a Master of Arts in Special and Additional Learning Needs at the University of East London.
