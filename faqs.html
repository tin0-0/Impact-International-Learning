About Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA)
Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA) is a scientific approach to understanding and improving behaviour. ABA uses evidence-based strategies to teach new skills, increase positive behaviours, and reduce behaviours that interfere with learning, communication, independence, and quality of life.
ABA can benefit individuals of all ages. It is commonly used to support individuals with autism, developmental disabilities, learning difficulties, behavioural challenges, and skill development needs in educational, clinical, workplace, and community settings.
No. Although ABA is widely recognised for supporting individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), its principles are applied across many areas including education, mental health, organisational behaviour management, parenting, sports coaching, and staff training.
Yes. ABA is one of the most researched and evidence-based approaches for behaviour change and skill development, supported by decades of scientific research.
ABA training can lead to a wide range of career opportunities, including:
  • Behaviour Technician
  • Autism Support Practitioner
  • Special Education Professional
  • Behaviour Analyst
  • Clinical Supervisor
  • Educational Consultant
  • Program Coordinator
  • School-Based Behaviour Specialist
  • Early Intervention Specialist
  • Behaviour Consultant

Enrolment & Entry Requirements
No. Our IBT and ABAT programmes are suitable for beginners and do not require previous ABA experience.
Generally, participants should:
  • Be at least 18 years old
  • Have basic English proficiency
  • Have completed secondary/high school education (recommended)
  • Have an interest in working with individuals who require behavioural or educational support
The IBA certification pathway offers both Bachelor's Degree and Master's Degree entry routes.
  • Bachelor's Degree Pathway: Candidates must hold a bachelor's degree in psychology, education, special education, social work, healthcare, or a related field.
  • Master's Degree Pathway: Candidates with a master's degree in a relevant discipline may pursue the advanced pathway toward IBA certification.

If you are unsure which pathway is suitable for you, contact us and we will guide you through the process.

International Recognition
Our training and certification pathways are aligned with internationally recognised credentialing boards, including QABA and IBAO. Both are international certification bodies whose credentials are held by professionals across multiple countries. However, recognition may vary depending on the requirements of individual employers, government agencies, and licensing authorities in a specific country. We encourage prospective students to review local regulations and contact us to discuss how these certifications support your career goals.
